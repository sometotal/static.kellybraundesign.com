Restaurant
Chicago, IL
2,500 sq.ft.
Interior Tenant Improvement
Completed in 2007
Performed work while at Gary Lee Partners
Published in Shelter, Gourmet, Elle Decor, DalCasa, Food Arts, Chicago Social, Chicago Magazine

Description
A restaurant in Chicagoâ€™s West Loop neighborhood in a former 1890 print shop serving New American food that is seasonal and local. The restaurant seats about 85 for dinner and 30 in the bar/lounge. The desire was for this restaurant to be modern yet celebrate tradition, as well as, pay homage to the industrial nature of the building and to Chicago. In addition, the client wanted guests to be able to come back to the restaurant time and again and have a completely different experience each time. The existing brick walls and freight elevator were maintained as a reminder of the past. The overall restaurant is treated as one space with subtle changes like chair/table style together with a single element that creates a unique statement defining different areas in turn creating new experiences. All of these varying elements are tied together with a cohesive color pallet. Through color, material and furniture selection an atmosphere was created that is warm, inviting and eclectic.Â  A harmonious blend of style & comfort and of past & present.

The restaurant opened with much success and even after a year, a prime weekend time slot for dinner requires reservation weeks in advance.

My Role
My involvement was developing ideas with the design team, furniture & material researching/tracking/documenting, design development detail drawings and a attending weekly construction site meetings. I was also a day to day contact for owner and contractor questions.

Furniture Showroom & Design
Chicago, IL
3,600 sq.ft.
Interior Tenant Improvement
Completed in 2005 & 2006
Performed work while at Gary Lee Partners

Â 

Description
A manufacturer of high quality contract wood furniture worked with Gary Lee Partners for a number of years developing a new complete furniture collection. In 2005, a casegood line was launched at NeoCon (interior design conference) along with prototypes of additional pieces from the collection. In order to accommodate the new line, the showroom was expanded to double the original size. The intent of the new space was to be a continuation of the old with maximum flexibility for changing furniture vignettes. Due to the successful response toward the casegood product, the remainder of the collection was launched at NeoCon 2006. The expansion provided a complete office solution including lounge seating, occasional tables, conference tables, guest seating and a unique panel system incorporating the flexibility of an office system line with the aesthetics of built in millwork. The collection has proven to be the most successful line that the manufacturer has ever produced. Both the showroom and the furniture designs are timeless and adaptable, with the ability to adjust from formal traditional to sleek modern settings.

My Role
I was involved in the entire design/construction process of the showroom renovation from field verification through construction administration as well as adjusting lighting on the furniture vignettes and purchasing accessories. As the product line developed, my involvement continued.Â  Beginning with drawings previously developed, I worked with the manufacturer on details through the fabrication process. In addition, assisted the manufacturers photographer with the shot selection of the new product and was present for design questions during NeoCon 2006.
